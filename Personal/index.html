Skin Lesion Classification

  • This project aims to use the benchmark dataset Ham10000 which consists of skin lesion images to conduct a statistical analysis under conditions where the dataset contains corrupt/noisy images and build models that obtains accurate results of image classification.
  • Using noisy images in the implementation of Skin Lesion classification is important because it helps to improve the model’s robustness and generalization performance. In the real world, images of skin lesions may contain various types of noise, such as blur, distortions, artifacts, and other types of interference.
  • By training the classification model on noisy images, we can make it more resistant to these types of noise and better able to generalize to unseen data.
  • Pre-trained models used - ResNet50, VGG16, DenseNet121, VGG19 and ResNet152.
  • Link to Source code and Report

His groundbreaking contributions to open-source software have had a profound impact on the tech industry and continue to inspire computer science students worldwide. Torvalds' revolutionary mindset, relentless pursuit of excellence, and dedication to collaboration motivate aspiring engineers like myself to push our boundaries and make our mark in the ever-evolving world of technology.

This shift in the tech landscape empowered individuals and organizations to harness the power of technology without the constraints of commercial licenses. Torvalds' vision serves as a reminder to computer science students that their innovations have the potential to shape the world, democratize knowledge, and improve lives on a global scale.
